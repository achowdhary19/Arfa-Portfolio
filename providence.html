Project Overview

Over a 5-month period, I worked with a small team of three to develop a modular web and mobile application for Providence Medical Services. Our application was designed to display AR volumetric videos created using Intel® RealSense™ cameras, enabling patients to learn physical therapy exercises at home and providing internal training materials for Providence's workforce across their 51 hospitals.

Technical considerations

This project had many moving parts. Some key aspects and their challenges included:


⁑ Designing an intuitive UI that maintained Providence's long standing brand identity
⁑ Building a user-friendly interface that expands access to specialized care, particularly for patients in remote locations who can't easily visit physical therapy facilities in person.
⁑ Creating an accessible interface that worked across both desktop and mobile devices
⁑ Integrating volumetric videos into an augmented reality environment, allowing patients to view exercises from multiple angles and better understand proper form and technique. These videos needed to be properly placed in the user's physical space, maintain proper scale and perspective, and remain responsive to user interactions
⁑ Developing a hosting solution that was more cost-effective than their existing third-party platform

My Role & Contributions

As a software developer on the project, my primary responsibility was designing and implementing the user interface. I used Figma to design a reusable UI component library that could adapt to differnet AR experiences. With that, I built a visual system that maintained Providence's established brand integrity and focused on accessibility for differnet users/use cases.

While I focused on UI design, our team collaborated closely on all aspects of development. I worked with my teammates to implement the augmented reality technology and ensurethat the interface we built + the volumetric videos fit properly with the AR environment. Using A-frame and 8th wall, we displayed the motion captured virtual environments in the user's real world. To add functionality to the app, we integrated the interactive button elements with the video player to ensure the appropriate response was triggered(i.e. pause/play, mute, captions, video scrubbing, looping).

Impact & Results

At the end of the project, Providence had functional, reusable prototypes that no longer rely on third party hosting, allowing them to pursue grants for additional features and expand the market for their volumetric videos.
The project was featured in Intel promotional materials and showcased at Providence's "SEAL: Stop Everything and Learn" week.
